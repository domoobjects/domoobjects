An About Page ◇ A Field Note ◇ Printed on Parchment

From Craft ◇ to Interface.

What this is

A digital exploration of Indian tribal crafts — translating tactile, handmade traditions into an interactive, considered interface. शिल्पात् अन्तरापृष्ठं यावत्

What it isn't

A conventional website. This is a narrative journey — moving from a broad geography of India to an intimate encounter with a single craft form.

read on

Craft is not a static artifact. It is a living system of knowledge — a way of thinking with the hand, with the land, and with time. This page exists to explain how we tried to translate that thinking into a screen without losing what made it worth translating.

Four Movements

The Shape of the Journey

मार्गः · map → craft → artifact → interaction

Geography as Storytelling

India, seen from far above.

The experience begins with a map of India, where each state becomes an entry point into its own regional craft traditions. Geography is not a decoration here — it is the scaffolding of the narrative.

Zoom into Gujarat, and the map reveals a curated set of villages where specific tribal crafts still live and breathe. The land leads. The story follows.

From Overview to Intimacy

Then closer, and closer still.

Selecting a craft pulls you into a focused narrative space — where its visual language, symbolism, and cultural context unfold through spatial and visual elements rather than paragraphs of description.

The craft is not explained at you. It is arranged around you, the way a village arranges itself around the thing it knows best.

Cloth, made into form, made into screen

A 3D object, inspired by Pithora.

At the center of the experience sits a digitized artifact — a 3D object inspired by Pithora painting. Rather than replicating the craft, the artifact abstracts its key elements into a physical form and its digital twin.

It is a bridge: between the handmade object and the interactive interface, between material practice and digital interpretation, between what the hand knows and what the screen remembers.

शिल्पं रूपं आवृत्तिं च

pithora · abstracted

Stamp · Layer · Compose

The hand returns.

The interaction model is drawn directly from the traditional process of block printing and ritual painting. Users engage by stamping patterns onto a digital surface, combining them, layering them, watching a composition emerge.

Every click is intentional. Every stamp is deliberate. The design echoes the physical act of applying paint, of pressing wood to cloth, of letting repetition become meaning.

मुद्रा · स्तरः · रचना

stamps · layered

The Artifact

An object that is also an interface.

A 3D artifact, inspired by Pithora painting, was designed and digitized as the central device of this project. It does not copy the craft — it abstracts it. Repetition, symbolic forms, and layered storytelling become a physical object, and that object becomes a digital representation.

This artifact is the pivot. Everything before it is about looking. Everything after it is about touching.

The Visual Language

Earth, fire, indigo, lime.

The palette draws from mud walls, natural pigments, and the colors that show up again and again across Indian tribal traditions. No accent neons. No pastels that came from nowhere. Hover a swatch to read its origin.

Lime-washed walls of a village home — calcium, crushed shell, cooled over time. The surface beneath every other color.

The Design Intent

Three things we believed.

I

Physical processes can inform digital ones.

The way you print a block of wood onto cloth can teach a touchscreen something about weight, intention, and sequence. Process is a teacher, not just a route to an output.

प्रक्रिया गुरुः
II

Culture is experienced, not merely observed.

A craft is not fully understood by reading about it. It reveals itself through rhythm, repetition, and use. The interface is built to be entered, not just scrolled.

अनुभवः एव ज्ञानम्
III

Interfaces can keep a sense of human touch.

Imperfection and irregularity are not bugs. They are essential to handmade traditions — and to any screen that wants to feel like it was made by people, for people.
