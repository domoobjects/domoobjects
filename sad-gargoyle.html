The Story

It Did the Job. It Paid the Price.

The gargoyle was put on the cathedral to protect everyone inside it. That was the agreement: carved from stone, positioned at the edge of everything, exposed to every storm and every century, standing guard over warmth and light happening behind it while it remained permanently outside of it.

It never got to go inside. It protected the congregation from the darkness and nobody ever thought to ask what the darkness was doing to the gargoyle.

This is the biography of the caretaker who never received care ?" the parent who held everyone together while quietly dissolving, the friend you called at 2am who never called anyone because calling felt like betraying the role they accepted without knowing they were accepting it.

The red is not anger. It is the color of a wound left open long enough to become permanent ?" decades of weather, of exposure, of being the thing between everyone else and the storm without anyone ever building a roof over the thing doing the protecting. One billion people globally live with a mental health condition. Most of them are standing at an edge, in the rain, making sure everyone inside is okay.
