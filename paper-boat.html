The Story

Folded From Something That Once Mattered.

There is a particular kind of childhood memory that lives in wet hands and kitchen floors — the moment you discovered that a flat piece of paper could become a vessel. Not a real one. Not a safe one. But something that floated, briefly, before the water won.

"The water always wins. The question is how long you float before it does."

DOMO's Paper Boat was designed in the same secret laboratory where we make everything we cannot explain. We began with a question: what object carries more weight than it appears to? The answer was immediate. Every paper boat is an act of faith — a document converted to a dream, a name converted to a voyage.

We chose our colors deliberately. The faded blue of a government form. The red of a stamped rejection. The white of blank pages and fresh starts and waved flags. The dark green of forests crossed at night. The glow-in-the-dark yellow for the ones who had to wait until it was dark enough to move.

Float one in a puddle. Float one in the bathtub where you used to play. Float one and remember that someone, somewhere, is floating the real thing. And the real thing does not have a bottom made of something stronger than paper.

Every paper boat begins as something else. We just decided to be honest about what.
